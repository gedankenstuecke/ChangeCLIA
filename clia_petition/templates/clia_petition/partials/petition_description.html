Change CLIA: allow sharing!

Stop prohibiting return of "non-CLIA" research data.

We call for CMS to issue a guidance
describing criteria for an exception to
the prohibition of return of results from
non-CLIA-certified research labs.

Why this matters.

The Center for Medicaid and Medicare Services (CMS) supports a controversially broad interpretation of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (CLIA), with a blanket prohibition on returning individual data from labs that are not CLIA certified.

As a result, a research participant can consent to sharing their data with anyone on earth – except themselves!

This bizarre outcome is legally contested. Others have described interpretations that do not result in a wholesale prohibition on return of data.
